# Double Entry Bookkeeping

Double-entry bookkeeping is an accounting method where every financial transaction affects at least two accounts.

## Basic Principle
Every transaction has:
- A Debit entry
- A Credit entry

Example:

If a company purchases equipment for $1000 in cash:

Debit: Equipment Account (+1000)  
Credit: Cash Account (-1000)

## Benefits
- Maintains accounting balance
- Reduces errors
- Enables accurate financial statements
